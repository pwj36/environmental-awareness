POLLUTION

According to WHO (n.d.), 1 in 7 people die because of pollution.

Pollution is the emission of harmful materials to the environment that results in adverse environmental changes. These toxic substances, known as pollutants, can be from nature but can also be created by human beings. Pollution is divided into many types, inclusive of air, water and land pollution. According to Sally (2014), among 56 million people worldwide that died in 2012, 9 million died because of pollution. Hence, it was reported that 1 in 7 people was killed by pollution. Additionally, it was discovered that pollution had caused chronic illnesses, such as neurological impairment and a wide range of diseases. For example, it was found that lead in children decreased their intelligence quotient (IQ). In locations where pollution is severe, newborns are born with abnormalities and lose up to 30 to 40 IQ points.

AIR POLLUTION

WHAT IS AIR POLLUTION?

According to Mackenzie and Turrentine (2021), air pollution is defined as the contamination of air due to harmful pollutants released into the air that are caused by chemical, physical or biological factors. It has been reported that every year, air pollution causes the death of nearly seven million people around the world. Currently, 9 out of 10 people are inhaling air that includes excessive amounts of contaminants. People in low and middle-income nations are more likely to be exposed to pollution.

CAUSES OF AIR POLLUTION


Burning Fossil Fuels

According to Deb (2019), most of today’s air pollution is caused by the combustion of fossil fuels. Industries use coal, oil, and gasoline as fuels to generate energy for power or transportation. The combustion of fossil fuels releases vast amounts of hazardous pollutants into the atmosphere, such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, et cetera. Additionally, nitrogen oxide emissions can result in acid rain and smog development.





Industrial Emissions

Industrial activities, such as factory emissions, can emit large volumes of pollutants into the air. Industries often employ coal and wood as their primary energy sources to manufacture commodities. This can release hazardous gases such as nitrogen oxide, sulfur dioxide, and carbon monoxide (Deb, 2019).






Wildfires

Burning grasses, trees and farm debris also contributes significantly to wildfires. Wildfires increase the amount of fine particulate matter in the air, combining other dangerous chemicals such as chemical gas and pollen, formatting smog (Deb, 2019).






Construction and Demolition

The rising population growth causes an increase in construction and demolition as the population grows. Construction sites require diesel-powered machinery such as excavators, backhoes, and cranes, which emit pollutants into the air (Guzder, 2019). Construction and demolition projects can often take a long time, resulting in high pollution emissions. Furthermore, construction and demolition also generate a lot of dust.





Transportation

The number of vehicles driven on the road is growing as the days pass. The majority of pollution by transportation is found in cities compared to rural regions, as automobile ownership rates are greater in cities. Vehicles operate on gasoline, which releases toxins into the atmosphere, such as carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons, nitrogen oxide, and fine particulate matter (Deb, 2019).




EFFECTS OF AIR POLLUTION

Several effects of air pollution includes (Stanley, 2022):

Irritation in Eyes

Air pollution can cause itchiness and irritation in eyes.

Throat irritation

Throat irritation can cause coughing or wheezing.




Difficulty Breathing

Pollution particles can cause airways to become inflamed, causing breathing difficulties.

Discomforts

Discomforts such as nausea and headache

Respiratory Disorders

Respiratory disorders like asthma and bronchitis

Reduced Visibility

Reduced visibility due to smog

WATER POLLUTION

WHAT IS WATER POLLUTION?

Water pollution is when toxins, most typically chemicals or microbes, enter rivers, oceans or water bodies, decreasing their quality and making them poisonous for people or other species (Denchak, 2022). It has been reported that approximately 2 million tonnes of sewage and waste from manufacturing sites and crop residues are released into the world's waterways (Sourgum Waste, n.d.). This has great potential to influence aquatic regions and our water bodies significantly.

CAUSES OF WATER POLLUTION


Deforestation

Deforestation refers to the destruction of forests for non-forest purposes (Stanley, 2022). This indicates that deforestation decreases the number of trees, which are essential for the filtration of water that goes into the river or sea (Lyons & Gartner, 2017). Hence, deforestation can result in reduced water filtering. Furthermore, heavy rain storms can result in flooding because there are no trees to anchor against erosion and absorb water. This can result in soil erosion, which destroys the top layer of soil dirt and pollutes the water. Lyons and Gartner (2017) also mentioned that sediments flow into streams and contaminate water when trees are disturbed, such as through deforestation.





Agriculture

Sagasta et al. (2017) have reported that agriculture is a major source of water contamination. Agriculture farms release vast amounts of chemicals, organic debris and sediments into rivers and lakes. Furthermore, agricultural output has expanded dramatically over the years, necessitating the extensive usage of pesticides and fertilizers. Additionally, excessive fertilizer use might result in surplus fertilizer being left unused in soil, which can flow into coastal waterways or rivers, causing eutrophication (Delp, n.d.). This proliferation of algae and plankton can lead to lower water quality and dissolved oxygen levels, resulting in water pollution.





Sewage

According to UNESCO (2017), over 80% of the world's sewage is released unprocessed and unregulated into water bodies. Population growth has spurred industrialization and development, which increased the amount of sewage created globally. Large volumes of wastewater in impoverished metropolitan nations are released straight into drainage systems without being treated, causing water contamination (UN Water, n.d.).





Plastic Pollution

Parker (2019) notes that approximately 8 million tonnes of plastic are released into coastal regions annually. Plastic and waste are transported downstream by rivers into oceans and accumulate together as they move down. Plastic also holds hazardous impacts, as chlorinated plastics may emit dangerous chemicals into aquifers or other water supply areas and the ecosystem (UNEP, 2021).





Oil Spills

Today, oil spills account for around 12% of all oil entering the ocean (Ohm, 2018). Oil spills from trucks or ships can cause severe problems as large amounts of oil are leaked into bodies of water. Oil leakage into the water does not dissolve, and hence can suffocate marine life species and block light from aquatic plants that require light to photosynthesise.




EFFECTS OF WATER POLLUTION

Several effects of water pollution includes (Denchak, 2022):

Contaminated Water

Pollutants that enter the water bodies can cause quality degradation of water.

Visual Alteration of Marine Waters

Litters and pollutants can wash up on shores, causing unpleasant visual alteration of the beach.



Put Dangers to Aquatic Animals

Plastic debris can choke or strangle aquatic animals.


Spread of Diseases

Spread of diseases such as cholera, giardia and typhoid due to unclean water.

Acidic Water Levels

Acidic water levels cause lower immune systems of aquatic life.

LAND POLLUTION

WHAT IS LAND POLLUTION?

Land pollution is the destruction of the Earth's land surfaces, above and beneath soil surfaces (Texas Disposal Systems, 2022). The degradation of land is caused by the buildup of waste items that pollute and damage groundwater and soil. Statistics show that nearly 75% of the Earth's land surface has been destroyed, with 3.2 billion people severely impacted by the polluted ground (Okafor, 2022).

CAUSES OF LAND POLLUTION


Litter

Littering is the act of inappropriate garbage disposal. Keep America Beautiful (2010) reported the presence of approximately 51 billion pieces of trash detected on US roads each year. The majority of litter is intentional, although some are inadvertent, such as unsecured things that fall from cars or garbage trucks (Texas Disposal Systems, 2022). Whether deliberate or unintentional, litter contaminates the land by producing chemicals and microparticles as it decomposes. Hence, causing damage to the soil and land.





Construction

As the population rises, the need to accommodate it increases as well. More land is required for the development of buildings and houses, which necessitates more construction activity (Texas Disposal Systems, 2022). Vast waste materials like timber, metal, plastic and bricks are not disposed of properly during these construction activities, which contributes to land contamination.





Mining

Mining involves the extraction of minerals and natural elements from land surfaces for a variety of uses, including the production of fuel for vehicles, the generation of power, and the sale of items such as gold and silver (Texas Disposal Systems, 2022). The extraction processes utilized for mining drain and degrade Earth's resources, bringing harm and polluting it.




EFFECTS OF LAND POLLUTION

Polluted Soil

Polluted soil, leading to infertile land.

Climate Change

Climate change that can result in flash floods and erratic rainfall.

Endangering Wildlife Species

Jeopardy and risks of extinction of wildlife species.

Habitat Shifts

Changes in an animal's environment resulting in habitat shifts.

Wildfires

Wildfires due to the drying up of contaminated regions.

Health Problems

Increased soil pollution can enter the body via the food supply chain and create health problems.
