OUR ENVIRONMENT

Did you know that 75% of the world's land surface is degraded?

The environment we live in now is currently at stake. The emission of harmful substances and greenhouse gases into our environment has resulted in pollution and climate change. Temperatures have risen since 1880 and will continue to increase if action to limit carbon emissions is not implemented (Lindsey & Dahlman, 2022). Scientists anticipate that death tolls will increase by nearly 5 million from intense heat induced by rising temperatures (Milman et al., 2021). World Animal Protection (2020) has identified climate change as one of the biggest threats to wildlife today. Climate change, such as heavy rainstorms, has caused sea and ocean temperatures to rise, threatening marine ecosystems. Additionally, climate change has caused significant shifts in ecoregions, resulting in the degradation of habitat and food supplies for terrestrial animals. This can increase the risk of animals being unable to adjust to their new surroundings.

THE PROBLEM

Pollution

Pollution is defined as the release of harmful compounds into the environment that can negatively impact it (National Geographic, 2022).

Pollution is divided into many types, inclusive of:

  1. Air
  2. Water
  3. Land

According to Vaughan (2022), 99% of people are affected by pollution caused by ourselves. It is time to take responsibility for our own actions.

Climate Change

Climate change is defined as a change in temperatures and atmospheric conditions that can last for an extended period (United Nations, n.d.).

Several consequences of climate changes include:

  1. Increase in temperatures
  2. Increase in sea levels
  3. Degradation of land

According to WWF (2018), about 50% of all plant and animal species are at risk of extirpation if carbon emissions continue to grow unabated.
