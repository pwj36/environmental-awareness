CLIMATE CHANGE

WHAT IS CLIMATE CHANGE?

Climate change is the shift in temperature and weather patterns for a protracted period (United Nations, n.d.). Some changes occur naturally, such as oscillations in the solar cycles. However, humans have become the primary cause of climate change ever since the 1800s. Human activities such as the combustion of fossil fuels generate greenhouse gas which traps heat from the sun, raising the atmosphere's temperatures.

The breakdown of greenhouse gases emitted into the atmosphere are as shown in the table below (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, 2014):

Greenhouse Gases Emissions
Greenhouse Gases Percentage (%)
Carbon dioxide 76
Methane 16
Nitrous Oxide 6
Fluorinated gases 2
Total 100

Among the significant greenhouse gas emitters are electricity, manufacturing companies, automobiles, farmlands and buildings (United Nations, n.d.). These emissions are not decreasing but instead increasing. The world is presently 1.1 degrees Celsius warmer than it was in 1800.

Climate change has caused severe droughts, water scarcity, wildfires, increased sea levels, flash floods, melting polar ice, catastrophic storms, and dwindling biodiversity. The process of climate change is currently causing severe droughts, water shortages, wildfires, increased sea levels, flash floods, melting ice caps and declining biodiversity.

CAUSES OF CLIMATE CHANGE


Generating Power

Urbanization means more fossil fuels are needed to generate power for the population. The use of fossil fuels to generate power and heat contributes significantly to global emissions. The majority of energy is produced by burning coal, oil, or gas, emitting greenhouse gases that trap the sun's heat.





Manufacturing Goods

The manufacturing industry is classified as a significant contributor to global greenhouse gas emissions. Manufacturing companies generate vast amounts of emissions, mainly from the combustion of coal, oil or gas, to provide fuel for the production of cement, metal, electronics and other items. Apart from that, mining and industrial operations also emit gases. These procedures can result in vast amounts of greenhouse gases being emitted.





Deforestation

Deforestation occurs when a large number of trees are chopped down. When trees are chopped down, they release all their accumulated carbon. According to research, over 12 million acres of forests are destroyed annually. Forest trees also assist in absorbing carbon dioxide. Hence, without them, carbon dioxide absorption would also decrease.





Automobiles

According to studies, automobiles are responsible for almost one-quarter of worldwide power-related carbon dioxide emissions. Researchers suggest that this figure will continue to rise in the following years. Automobiles, including vars, lorries, and trucks, operate on fossil fuels and thus are significant generators of greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide emissions.




EFFECTS OF CLIMATE CHANGE

Several effects of land pollution include (United Nations, n.d.; Mimura, 2013):

Rise in Temperatures

Rise in atmospheric and ocean temperatures

Rising Sea Levels

Rise in sea levels due to thermal expansion and melting of ice.

Food Shortages

Food shortages due to extreme weather conditions

Unpredictable Weather Patterns

Unpredictable weather patterns that can increase drought and flash floods

Health Risks

Health risks from extreme weather conditions, diseases from pollution, etc.

Survival Risks of Animals

Survival risks due to adverse climate changes
